Overview

I am a sophomore at Rensselaer Polytechnic Institute pursuing a dual major in Cognitive Science and Information Technology & Web Science (ITWS). I am interested in understanding how people think, learn, and interact with technology, and in building systems that are structured, intuitive, and human-centered.

My work and coursework span research, programming, and web development, with a strong emphasis on communication, service, and continuous learning. I enjoy environments where I can collaborate, ask questions, and translate complex ideas into usable solutions.

I am involved in research that focuses on attention, executive function, learning, and human–computer interaction. Through coursework and lab-based work, I am gaineing experience with experimental design, research ethics, and analyzing human behavior in structured settings.

Through RCOS-related work, I contribute to research-oriented and open-source projects that emphasize usability, documentation clarity, and lowering barriers to entry for new users. I am working to rebuild the website that provides instructions on the robots for the robotics students.

Recent Projects

108 Divya Desam Interactive Web Project Web Development
Designed and developed an interactive web-based project that organizes and presents information about the 108 Divya Desam(108 landmarks in India) using structured HTML/CSS and geospatial concepts. The project focused on clear information architecture, accessibility, and presenting cultural and research-based content in a navigable digital format. I also recorded short audio explanations and details about each landmark.
